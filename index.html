Bowed String Instruments of the World

Violin

Place of origin: Italy

Number of strings: 4

Tuning: Western notes G D A E

Construction: Maple body, ebony fingerboard, nylon strings

History: The violin has it's roots in 16th-century Italian string instruments, but it didn't look like it does today until 19th century. In Europe, it served as the basis for the development of other stringed instruments used in Western classical music, such as the viola. The violin itself was developed after the viola da gamba, an older Western string instrument.

Description: The violin is the soprano member of the family of string instruments that includes the viola and cello. It is the smallest member of the family with the highest pitch, and it often used as a solo instrument in Western orchestras. It is tuned in fourths and is one of the most popular string instruments of Western music. Violins are also called "fiddles" in Bluegrass music and other folk musical genres.
